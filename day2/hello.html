Manamey
"Manamey" is a 2024 Telugu romantic comedy-drama directed by Sriram Adittya, featuring Sharwanand, Krithi Shetty, and child actor Vikram Aditya in pivotal roles.
Set against the picturesque backdrop of London, the film tells the story of Vikram, a carefree and fun-loving man whose life takes an unexpected turn when he is entrusted with the responsibility of raising a child named Kushi after the untimely death of his close friend.
Alongside him is Subhadra, a disciplined and responsible woman who also has a deep connection to the child’s family. Initially at odds with each other due to their clashing personalities and parenting styles, Vikram and Subhadra slowly learn to cooperate, adapt, and eventually grow closer as they co-parent the child.
The narrative gently explores themes of love, responsibility, and transformation as Vikram evolves from a flirtatious bachelor into a devoted father figure.
The film is visually rich, boasting lush cinematography that captures London’s charm, and features a soothing soundtrack composed by Hesham Abdul Wahab that complements the film’s emotional and romantic undertones.
However, despite its heartfelt premise and vibrant performances, particularly from Sharwanand and Krithi Shetty, "Manamey" has received mixed reviews due to its predictable plot, dragging second half, and over-reliance on formulaic storytelling.
Critics have pointed out that while the movie succeeds in delivering feel-good moments and family-friendly content, it falls short in terms of narrative depth and originality. The film’s technical aspects, such as editing and visual effects, also received lukewarm responses, and some viewers felt that the story served more as a travelogue than a tightly written script.
Nevertheless, for audiences seeking a light-hearted emotional journey with strong visuals and a dose of sentimental charm, "Manamey" offers a pleasant, if not particularly groundbreaking, cinematic experience.

Qalb
"Qalb", in Islamic spirituality—especially within Sufi tradition—refers not merely to the physical heart but to the metaphysical center of consciousness, intuition, and divine connection within a human being.
It is considered the spiritual heart, the seat of intention, sincerity, and perception of truth.
Unlike the mind, which is associated with logic and reason, the qalb is believed to be the vessel through which one perceives divine realities and connects with God.
It is inherently dynamic, constantly turning between remembrance (dhikr) and forgetfulness, guidance and misguidance—hence its root meaning in Arabic, “to turn” or “to flip.”
A purified qalb, cultivated through acts of worship, ethical living, remembrance of God, and detachment from worldly distractions, becomes illuminated and receptive to divine light and wisdom (ma‘rifah).
However, if neglected, it can be veiled by spiritual diseases like arrogance, hypocrisy, and heedlessness, leading to a hardened or darkened state where truth is no longer recognized.
In the Qur'an, a sound heart (qalbun salīm) is described as the ultimate key to salvation. Thus, spiritual practices in Islam aim not just at outward compliance, but at refining and awakening the qalb, transforming the individual from within so they may live in harmony with divine will and attain closeness to the Creator.

Friendship

It offers a safe space where vulnerabilities are accepted, where silence can be comfortable, and where joy is amplified simply by being together.
Friends are those rare souls who walk beside us through the highs and lows of life, offering encouragement in our struggles, laughter in our happiness, and perspective in our confusion.
Unlike many other relationships, friendship is chosen voluntarily, and that very choice deepens its beauty—it is a relationship born of love, not obligation.
In times of hardship, a true friend becomes a source of strength, a reminder that we are not alone.
They challenge us to grow, hold up a mirror to our better selves, and stand with us even when the world seems to turn away.
Friendship transcends distance, differences, and time, often growing stronger with shared memories, inside jokes, and moments of silent understanding.
In a world that often moves too fast and prioritizes success over sincerity, friendship remains a gentle reminder of the human need for connection, belonging, and unconditional support.

Emoji
"Emoji" is a 2024 Telugu-language romantic comedy that creatively explores modern digital relationships through the lens of a lighthearted yet emotionally resonant narrative.
Set in a contemporary urban backdrop, the film follows the quirky and charming journey of its lead characters, played by Nithiin and Anupama Parameswaran, who find themselves entangled in a romance that begins and evolves largely through the medium of emojis and online communication.
As they navigate misunderstandings, emotional highs and lows, and the complexities of virtual bonding versus real-world emotions, the story unfolds with both humor and heartfelt moments.
The film cleverly uses the concept of emojis not just as visual tools for humor or relatability, but as symbols of the unspoken emotions people often mask in the digital age. With vibrant visuals, upbeat music, and a fast-paced narrative, "Emoji" captures the essence of millennial and Gen Z communication styles while also delving into deeper themes of authenticity, vulnerability, and human connection.
The chemistry between the lead actors adds warmth and depth to the film, while the screenplay balances comedy with introspective drama, ensuring that the storyline remains engaging.
Directed by Sandeep Raj, the movie skillfully mirrors how technology shapes modern love stories and underscores the idea that behind every cheerful emoji lies a person with genuine feelings, struggles, and desires.
Ultimately, "Emoji" is a charming, feel-good entertainer that reflects today’s digitally driven world while reminding viewers of the timeless beauty of emotional honesty and face-to-face connection.

Malli Rava
"Malli Raava" is a heartfelt Telugu romantic drama released in 2017, directed by Gowtam Tinnanuri in his debut feature, and it delicately portrays the emotional evolution of love through the passage of time.
The film stars Sumanth and Aakanksha Singh as Karthik and Anjali, childhood sweethearts whose relationship unfolds across three distinct timelines—1999, 2012, and 2017—offering a non-linear narrative that captures the subtle shifts in their connection as they grow from teenagers into adults.
The story begins with a young Karthik instantly falling for Anjali during their school days, only to be separated by circumstances, and their lives cross paths again years later, rekindling the bond that once existed.
However, just as they are about to get married, Anjali abruptly walks away, leaving Karthik to grapple with heartbreak and confusion.
The film is devoid of typical commercial tropes and melodrama, opting instead for a quiet, introspective tone that emphasizes the beauty and pain of unfulfilled love.
Sumanth delivers a mature, restrained performance that highlights Karthik’s internal struggles, while Aakanksha Singh impresses as the emotionally guarded Anjali, whose hesitations and fears feel deeply human.
Their chemistry feels organic, and the transitions between past and present are handled with sensitivity, revealing how people change with time, yet some emotions remain timeless.
The music by Shravan Bharadwaj enhances the emotional depth of the narrative, with soulful melodies that echo the film’s themes of longing and lost opportunities.
Though some viewers may find the pacing a bit slow or the structure slightly confusing, "Malli Raava" resonates strongly with those who value realistic storytelling, emotional nuance, and nostalgic romance.
It’s a film that speaks to anyone who has experienced the ache of love delayed, the sting of what-ifs, and the enduring hope that perhaps some hearts are meant to find their way back to each other, no matter how long it takes.
